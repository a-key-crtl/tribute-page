Alan Turing

The father of modern computer science

Here's a time line of Alan Turing's life:


  • 1912 - Born in Maida Vale, London.

  • 1926 - At age of 13, Turing first showed signs of intelligence and potential noticed at Sherborne School.

  • 1931 - Studies from 1931-1934 at King's College, in Cambridge. First in his class and an honor student in field of mathematics.

  • 1936 - Studies mathematics and cryptology at Princeton University

  • 1940 - Creates Bombe which was a cryptanalytic machine used to decrypt the Enigma-enciphered messages being sent from enemies.

  • 1946 - Presents paper in February showcases first detailed design of a stored program computer setup.

  • 1948 - Created LU decomposition method still used today to solve matrix equations. Appointed a position in mathematics at University of Manchester worked on software for one of the earliest stored-program computers.

  • 1950 - Turing test created to compare the intelligence of a machine/computer to the intelligence of a human brain.

  • 1952 - Caught in a relationship with a 19 year old man. Convicted for indecency since homosexual acts were considered criminal offences in the United Kingdom at the time. Led to his downward spiral right before his suicide.

  • 1954 - Turing commits suicide in his house where his housekeeper finds him dead.
